Code Across America 2014:
Miami Open Data Jam

FEBRUARY 22, 2014 | 12 - 2 P.M. | THE LIGHT BOX, WYNWOOD

Join Code for Miami, a local, volunteer brigade of Code for America, to brainstorm ideas to make Miami-Dade County's public data more transparent, more accessible and easier to use.

What is an open data policy?

An open data policy is a local law that gives citizens quick and inexpensive access to download and use the raw public data that governments collect. Historically, access to this data has only been available via formal data requests to government entities, sometimes at significant expense. Across the nation, cities passing resolutions, executive orders, and ordinances to make local data more accessible and useful:

You can learn more about Open Data Policies across America at sunlightfoundation.com/policy/local/.

Why does local open data matter?

We think that open access to government data is a vital issue. It is the tool of government transparency for our time, the foundation of community and civic technology innovation, and an enabler of democratic participation in government processes.

What are we doing?

We're hostng Miami Open Data Jam, a collaborative brainstorming session to help create an open data policy for Miami-Dade County.
